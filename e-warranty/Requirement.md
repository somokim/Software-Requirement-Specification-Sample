### Conflicts Detected

| Conflict ID | Between Stakeholders | Description |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------|
| C01 | S14 (Marketing Manager) vs S13 (Legal / Compliance Officer) | Marketing wants to capture additional customer data at registration (usage context, channel preference, purchase source) and use expiry notifications for upsell and repurchase prompts. Legal requires strict separation of warranty processing and marketing consent, with no bundled opt-in. The conflict is not irreconcilable but requires an explicit consent architecture and clear rules on what data can be used for which purpose — this must be resolved in requirements before the registration form is designed. |
| C02 | S11 (IT Security Officer) vs S03 (Warranty Operations Manager) | Security requires comprehensive audit logging, MFA, strict role-based access controls, and a mandatory pre-go-live penetration test — all of which add implementation time and operational friction. Operations prioritises speed of daily use and minimal barriers for staff. Conflict is most acute around MFA for internal users (Security: mandatory; Operations: not explicitly requested and likely to resist if it slows claim processing). Requires a governance decision from S06 (Business Sponsor). |
| C03 | S10 (Data Analyst) vs S03 (Warranty Operations Manager) | Data Analyst requires data to be exported to an external data warehouse (Snowflake) for meaningful analysis, stating that in-system reporting alone is insufficient. Operations Manager expects operational reporting to be fully available within the system itself. These are not mutually exclusive but require both in-system operational reporting AND an external export capability — both must be built, and the scope of each must be clearly delineated to avoid under-building either. |
| C04 | S08 (System Architect) vs S07 (IT Project Manager) | The Architect wants several technical decisions confirmed before detailed requirements are written — specifically the product catalogue integration approach, customer authentication mechanism, and data residency decision — because the requirements themselves differ depending on the architectural answer. The IT PM has a tight timeline (approximately 24 weeks to go-live) and cannot afford to delay requirements work pending architectural decisions. Resolution requires a rapid time-boxed architectural decision workshop, with decisions documented as constraints before detailed requirements are finalised. |
| C05 | S06 (Business Sponsor) vs S13 (Legal / Compliance Officer) | The Business Sponsor has confirmed that ERP and payment/refund processing are explicitly out of scope. Legal requires the system to hold immutable proof of purchase records and support point-in-time audit queries — which may require structured purchase transaction data that would typically originate from a payment or ERP system. If purchase data is customer-supplied only and not verified against a transaction system, the evidential strength of that data in a legal dispute is reduced. Requires a Legal sign-off on the adequacy of customer-supplied proof of purchase as sole evidentiary basis. |
| C06 | S14 (Marketing Manager) vs S01 (Customer / Product Owner) | Marketing wants to include upsell and repurchase content in warranty expiry notifications. The customer stakeholder explicitly stated they want communications that are relevant and not excessive — specifically one email per event and no "warranty newsletter." Embedding promotional content in what the customer expects to be a service notification risks eroding trust and increasing unsubscribe rates. Requires a clearly defined policy on which notifications are service communications and which are marketing communications, with the latter requiring separate opt-in consent (reinforcing C01). |
| C07 | S05 (Product Manager) vs S02 (Warranty Claims Processor) | Product Manager requires standardised fault category codes for trend analysis. Claims Processor currently uses and relies on free-text fault descriptions, and any transition to structured fault codes will require training and potentially slow processing during adoption. The conflict is primarily a change management issue rather than a technical one, but the requirement for structured fault codes (FR-016) must not be compromised to accommodate current habits — the resolution is adequate training and a transition plan, not a hybrid free-text solution. |
| C08 | S08 (System Architect) vs S06 (Business Sponsor) | The Architect flags that the customer portal requires a standalone customer authentication mechanism (external users are not in Azure AD), which is a non-trivial build item. The Business Sponsor's hard go-live deadline of early September, combined with the 24-week timeline, creates pressure to simplify or defer features. If scope pressure builds, authentication design is a likely target for shortcutting — which Security (S11) would not accept. This tension must be surfaced early as a schedule risk. |
| C09 | S07 (IT Project Manager) vs S03 (Warranty Operations Manager) | The IT PM has negotiated only 4 hours per fortnight of the Operations Manager's time for requirements review, which the PM himself acknowledged is likely insufficient. The Operations Manager is the primary sign-off authority for operational and functional requirements and also the most operationally constrained stakeholder. Inadequate review time risks requirements being signed off with unresolved ambiguities, or sign-off being withheld and delaying the project. Requires escalation to S06 (Business Sponsor) to formally protect the Operations Manager's time for requirements activities. |
| C10 | S10 (Data Analyst) vs S06 (Business Sponsor) | The Business Sponsor has set a warranty registration rate target of 60% of units sold as a success metric. Achieving this metric requires linking registration data to sales volume data — which the Data Analyst confirmed does not currently exist in a linkable form. FR-071 addresses this partially, but the source of sales volume data is unresolved. If sales data cannot be imported or maintained within the system, the Business Sponsor's primary success metric cannot be reported from the system. Requires a decision on how units-sold data will be supplied to the system. |
