### Questionnaire for: Customer / Product Owner (S01)

*Persona context: A consumer who has purchased a product and needs to register it, monitor warranty coverage, or lodge a claim. What matters most is simplicity, speed, and confidence that their product is protected without bureaucratic friction.*

1. Walk me through the last time you registered a product warranty — what did you actually have to do, step by step, from the moment you opened the box?
2. When you needed to check whether a product was still under warranty, how did you find that information, and how long did it take?
3. Have you ever tried to make a warranty claim? If so, what documents or proof did you need to gather, and where did that process break down or frustrate you?
4. What details about a product do you typically struggle to locate when asked to provide them during registration (e.g. serial number, purchase date, model code)?
5. If you submitted a warranty claim and heard nothing back for several days, what would you do — who would you contact and through what channel?
6. Have you ever lost a paper warranty card, missed a registration deadline, or been told your warranty was invalid for a reason you didn't expect? What happened?
7. When it comes to receiving updates about your warranty — expiry reminders, claim progress notifications — what channel do you actually want to hear from (email, SMS, app notification) and how often is too often?
8. What would make you feel genuinely confident that your warranty claim was being handled seriously and not lost in a queue somewhere?
9. If you could change one thing about how warranty registration and claims work today, what would it be?

### Questionnaire for: Warranty Claims Processor (S02)

*Persona context: An internal staff member who spends the majority of their working day reviewing incoming warranty claims, validating eligibility, and processing approvals or rejections. What matters most is having accurate data in front of them quickly so they can make the right decision without chasing information across multiple systems or paper files.*

1. Take me through exactly what happens from the moment a warranty claim lands on your desk or in your queue — what is the first thing you check, and what are each of the steps you follow before you can approve or reject it?
2. What information do you need to verify in order to determine whether a claim is eligible, and where do you currently go to find each piece of that information?
3. When a customer's details don't match what's on record — for example, the serial number doesn't align, or the purchase date is disputed — what do you do? Who do you involve, and how long does that typically take to resolve?
4. How do you currently manage your claims queue? Do you work through items in order, prioritise by age, product type, or something else entirely?
5. What are the most common reasons you reject a claim, and are those decisions always clear-cut, or are there grey areas where you have to use your own judgement?
6. Describe a scenario where a claim went significantly wrong — either you made an error, something was missed, or a customer escalated. What caused it and how was it resolved?
7. Are there any manual steps you perform today — copy-pasting data, sending templated emails, updating spreadsheets — that you believe serve no real purpose other than filling a gap in the current system?
8. What does a good day look like in terms of throughput — how many claims would you expect to process in a day without feeling overwhelmed — and what typically derails that?
9. Is there any information you wish you had access to at the point of reviewing a claim that you currently have to go and ask someone else for?

### Questionnaire for: Warranty Operations Manager (S03)

*Persona context: A department manager responsible for the performance of the warranty team, adherence to SLAs, and the quality of claim outcomes. What matters most is visibility across the team's workload, the ability to intervene early when something is off-track, and being able to demonstrate operational improvement to senior leadership.*

1. How do you currently monitor your team's claims processing workload — do you have a live view of queue volumes, pending items, and individual staff throughput, or are you relying on end-of-day reports and verbal updates?
2. What SLAs or turnaround commitments currently govern warranty claim processing, and how do you know when one is about to be breached before it actually happens?
3. When a high volume of claims comes in — for example, following a product recall or a seasonal peak — how do you manage capacity and redistribute work across your team today?
4. Which decisions are you making daily that you currently don't have the data to make confidently, and what do you have to do instead to compensate?
5. How are escalated or disputed claims currently handled — what triggers an escalation, who gets involved, and what's the documented process versus what actually happens in practice?
6. When something goes wrong operationally — a batch of claims processed incorrectly, an SLA missed across the board, a processing error that affects multiple customers — how do you find out, and what does the recovery process look like?
7. What does your current reporting cycle look like? How often do you produce reports, to whom, in what format, and how long does it take to compile that data?
8. If you had complete, real-time visibility of your team's operations tomorrow, what are the three specific numbers you would want on your screen first thing every morning?
9. What does the current process cost your team in terms of administrative overhead that you believe should not exist once this system is in place?

### Questionnaire for: Customer Service Representative (S04)

*Persona context: A front-line support agent handling inbound enquiries from customers who have questions about their warranty status, want to lodge a claim, or are frustrated with a previous interaction. What matters most is being able to resolve a customer's issue in a single interaction without having to put them on hold, escalate unnecessarily, or call back later with information.*

1. When a customer calls or messages asking about their warranty, what information do you need to pull up their record, and how many different places do you currently have to look to find it?
2. Walk me through what happens when a customer wants to submit a claim through you rather than doing it themselves — do you enter information on their behalf, or do you direct them elsewhere, and what problems arise from that process?
3. What are the most common warranty-related questions customers ask that you currently cannot answer immediately because the information simply isn't available to you?
4. How do you handle situations where a customer insists their product is under warranty but your records suggest it isn't — what steps do you follow and how is that dispute documented?
5. What percentage of your warranty-related contacts would you estimate are customers following up on something they already submitted because they haven't received any communication about it?
6. Describe the worst type of warranty-related customer interaction you regularly face — what makes it so difficult to resolve, and what would need to exist in a system to make it manageable?
7. Are there any customer commitments you make during a call — "I'll email you a confirmation," "someone will be in touch within 48 hours" — that you then have to manually track or follow up on yourself?
8. What information would you want visible on a customer's profile the moment you open it, before they've even had a chance to explain why they're contacting you?

### Questionnaire for: Product Manager (S05)

1. How do you currently receive information about warranty claims related to your products — is it a regular report, an ad hoc request to someone in operations, or something you pull yourself?
2. What product-level data points would allow you to determine whether a fault is a one-off incident or an emerging systemic issue that warrants escalation?
3. Have you ever discovered a product quality problem through warranty claims data after it had already been affecting customers for a significant period? What delayed that discovery?
4. When you need to compare warranty claim rates across two product variants or across different manufacturing batches, what does that process look like today and how long does it take?
5. Are there specific warranty failure categories or fault codes that you would want standardised and tracked consistently so that you can trend them over time?
6. What do you do today when warranty data suggests a product is underperforming — what is the escalation path, and what evidence do you need to trigger an internal investigation?
7. Is there any warranty insight you believe your organisation is currently failing to capture or act on, and what commercial impact has that blind spot had?
8. How would you want warranty reporting surfaced to you — a dashboard you access yourself, a scheduled report emailed to you, or an alert when a threshold is crossed?
